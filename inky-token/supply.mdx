## Hard cap

The maximum supply of INKY that can ever exist is **200,000,000** tokens. This cap is enforced at the smart contract level and is immutable: no function exists in the contract to increase it.

To verify: open the [INKY contract on BscScan](https://bscscan.com/address/0x75a320c97205dd2e70e09085d1408c73a73d4d8f#readContract), call the `cap()` function.

## Initial mint

At deploy, **10,000,000 INKY** were minted and sent to the Initial Mintage Vault ([`0x19d24adf08d2c169667353957adb02e7aa4127a9`](https://bscscan.com/address/0x19d24adf08d2c169667353957adb02e7aa4127a9#code)). This vault custodies the initial supply and releases tokens according to the Inkryptus economic plan.

The Initial Mintage Vault is a **Gnosis Safe** (multisig) proxy contract, meaning fund releases require multiple signatures. As of the last on-chain read, it still holds **9,000,000 INKY** of the original 10M mint.

A **UniversalConversionVault** ([`0xbf2ae077...`](https://bscscan.com/address/0xbf2ae077e85e43198a84903fc0af1ba4b2dcdefc)) also holds ~114,727 INKY and manages token conversions with daily limits.

> **Note on holder distribution**: Inkryptus is a custodial platform. Most user balances are held inside platform contracts (Wallet, Products Vault, ConversionVault), which is why a small number of contract addresses concentrate a large share of the supply. Individual user balances exist within those contracts but are not visible as separate holders on BscScan. The PancakeSwap pool is public, so some users also hold INKY outside the platform.
