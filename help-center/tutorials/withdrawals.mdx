## Send assets (external withdrawal)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select asset" icon="circle" title-type="p">
    Open the asset in your wallet and tap **Send**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste destination" icon="copy" title-type="p">
    Paste the destination wallet address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm BEP20" icon="check" title-type="p">
    Verify that the destination wallet supports BNB Smart Chain (BEP20).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter amount" icon="edit" title-type="p">
    Enter the withdrawal amount. Observe the minimum (US$6) and the fixed network fee (US$3).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Validate with email code" icon="mail" title-type="p">
    Tap **Confirm** and enter the security code sent to your email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track transaction" icon="activity" title-type="p">
    Monitor progress through the transaction hash in app history.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="warning">
  Before confirming, review address, asset, and BEP20 network. Send a test transaction with a small amount when needed.
</Callout>

---

## How to find the TXID (hash)

- **External transfers:** open asset details and locate the transaction hash.
- **Internal actions:** check the transaction ID in app history.
- If you cannot find it, contact support with operation details.

---

## Example: Send USDT to Binance

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get Binance address" icon="copy" title-type="p">
    In Binance: **Wallet > Assets > Add Funds > Deposit Crypto**. Select USDT, choose BSC (BEP20), and copy the deposit address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send from Inkryptus" icon="arrow-right" title-type="p">
    In Inkryptus: **Wallet > Assets > Tether (USDT) > Send**. Paste the Binance address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter amount and confirm" icon="check-circle" title-type="p">
    Tap **Next**, enter amount (or MAX), tap **Send**, and validate with email code.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Expected result:** USDT arrives in Binance for conversion to local currency.

## Withdraw to local currency (off-ramp via Binance)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm receipt" icon="check" title-type="p">
    In Binance, verify USDT arrived in **Wallet > Spot**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Convert to fiat" icon="dollar-sign" title-type="p">
    Convert USDT to your local currency (BRL, USD, EUR, etc.).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Withdraw" icon="arrow-right" title-type="p">
    Go to **Wallet > Spot > Withdraw** and choose your method (PIX, SEPA, etc.).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="info">
  Binance may require KYC and additional checks depending on your region.
</Callout>

## Recover INKY sent to an exchange

If you sent INKY directly to Binance (or another exchange) without converting to USDT first, the tokens will not be credited automatically because INKY is not listed on that exchange. In most cases, recovery is possible by requesting a manual return from the exchange.

### Step 1: Open a support ticket with the exchange

For Binance, follow these steps in the app:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open menu" icon="menu" title-type="p">
    Tap the hamburger menu (top left corner).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find support" icon="help-circle" title-type="p">
    Tap **More Services**, scroll to **Help and Support**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select issue" icon="alert-circle" title-type="p">
    Tap **Self-Service**, then **Crypto Deposits Not Received**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open manual ticket" icon="message-circle" title-type="p">
    Tap **Contact Support** to open a manual ticket.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once the ticket is open, request the return of your INKY tokens to your Inkryptus deposit address via BEP20. Include the following information:

| Information | Where to find it |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Transaction hash (TxID) | Inkryptus app: **Home > INKY > History** > select the withdrawal |
| Exchange BEP20 address (origin) | The address you sent INKY to |
| Inkryptus deposit address (destination for refund) | Inkryptus app: **Home > INKY > Receive** |
| INKY token contract (BEP20) | `0x75a320C97205DD2e70e09085d1408c73A73D4d8f` |

### Step 2: If the exchange asks whether the address is a contract

Share the following message with them:

<Callout kind="info">
  The Inkryptus deposit address is a valid custodial wallet address generated on the Inkryptus platform. It is fully capable of receiving BEP20 tokens. It may be identified as a contract address due to the custodial infrastructure. This is expected and does not affect the transfer.
</Callout>

### Step 3: If the exchange requests official confirmation from Inkryptus

Contact Inkryptus support. We will provide a confirmation message you can forward to the exchange.

### Step 4: After the tokens are back in your Inkryptus wallet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm balance" icon="check" title-type="p">
    Go to **Wallet > Assets > INKY** and verify the returned tokens are visible.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Convert to USDT" icon="repeat" title-type="p">
    Tap **Sell** to convert INKY to USDT.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send USDT to the exchange" icon="arrow-right" title-type="p">
    Send the USDT to your exchange address via BEP20. This is the recommended workflow for moving value out of Inkryptus.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="warning">
  Always convert INKY to USDT before sending to external exchanges. INKY is a utility token within the Inkryptus ecosystem and is not listed on major exchanges.
</Callout>
